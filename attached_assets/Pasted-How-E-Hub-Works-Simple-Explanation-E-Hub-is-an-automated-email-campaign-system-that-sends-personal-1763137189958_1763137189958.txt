How E-Hub Works (Simple Explanation)
E-Hub is an automated email campaign system that sends personalized cold outreach emails to potential customers (cannabis dispensaries) using AI to write every message.

The Big Picture
Instead of manually writing hundreds of similar emails, you:

Create a sequence (just give it a name like "Cold Outreach Q1 2025")
Chat with AI to plan your campaign strategy (what you want to say, who you're targeting, your tone)
Set step delays (how many days between Email 1, Email 2, Email 3, etc.)
Add recipients (import contacts from Google Sheets)
Activate the sequence
Then the system automatically generates unique emails for each person and sends them at smart times.

How AI Generates an Email (Step-by-Step)
Step 1: You Plan the Campaign Strategy
You chat with an AI assistant about your campaign goals
Example conversation:
You: "I'm reaching out to dispensaries to offer hemp wick products"
AI: "What's your main value proposition?"
You: "We help them reduce plastic waste and improve sustainability"
AI: "Should I emphasize eco-friendly messaging or cost savings?"
You: "Focus on sustainability first, then mention it saves money"
This conversation gets saved as your campaign strategy.

Step 2: The System Knows About the Recipient
For each contact, the system already has:

Their name (e.g., "Sarah Johnson")
Their email
Their dispensary info (name, location, business hours)
A brief summary about their business (pulled from Leafly or your database)
Step 3: AI Writes a Unique Email
When it's time to send, the AI gets three things:

Your campaign strategy (the conversation you had about tone, messaging, goals)
Recipient context (their name, business info)
Instructions (write short emails, don't sound spammy, use specific formatting)
The AI then generates:

A unique subject line (3-5 words, under 35 characters)
A personalized email body (under 70 words for first email, even shorter for follow-ups)
Step 4: AI Makes Each Email Different
The AI doesn't use templates - it writes fresh content every time. Two emails might have:

Different opening sentences
Different ways of explaining your product
Different calls-to-action
But they all follow your campaign strategy
Step 5: Follow-Ups Work Automatically
If Email 1 doesn't get a reply:

After the delay you set (e.g., 3 days), AI writes a follow-up email
It references the previous email: "Following up on my message from a few days ago..."
The subject line uses "Re:" to keep the email thread together
It adds new value instead of repeating the intro
Step 6: The System Stops When Appropriate
Email sending stops automatically when:

The recipient replies (detected by checking Gmail)
The sequence is complete (sent all steps)
You manually pause or remove them
Smart Timing Example
Let's say you set:

Sequence: 3 emails with 0 days, 3 days, 7 days delays
Sending hours: 9 AM - 2 PM (your timezone)
Daily limit: 200 emails
The system will:

Email 1: Schedule immediately (but respects recipient's business hours and your sending window)
Email 2: Wait 3 days, then send during their morning hours (e.g., 1 hour after they open)
Email 3: Wait 7 more days, same smart timing
All sends are spaced out throughout the day to avoid spam flags, and nothing goes out on weekends if you enable "skip weekends."

Real Example Flow
You add "Green Leaf Dispensary" to a sequence:

System checks their timezone (Pacific) and business hours (9 AM - 9 PM)
Calculates best send time: 10 AM Pacific (1 hour after they open)
AI generates Email 1:
Subject: "Quick Question"
Body: "Hi, I found your contact on Leafly. We help dispensaries like Green Leaf reduce plastic waste with our hemp wick alternative. Would you be open to a quick call this week?"
Email sends automatically at 10 AM Pacific
System monitors Gmail for replies
After 3 days with no reply, AI generates Email 2:
Subject: "Re: Quick Question"
Body: "Following up on my email from last week. Wanted to share that we just helped a similar dispensary cut their plastic usage by 40%. Would 15 minutes work to discuss?"
If they reply at any point, system stops sending and marks them as "replied"
The key insight: You write the strategy once, AI writes hundreds of unique emails following that strategy. No copy-paste, no templates, every email feels personal and natural.
