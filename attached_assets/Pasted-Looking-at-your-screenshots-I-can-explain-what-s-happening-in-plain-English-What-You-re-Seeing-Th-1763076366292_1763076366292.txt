Why This Happens
The Step Delays Configuration
You configured:

Email 1: Send immediately (0 days delay)
Email 2: Send 3 days AFTER Email 1
Email 3: Send 8 days AFTER Email 2
Email 4: Send 10 days AFTER Email 3
This is per-person, not across all people. Each recipient gets their own timeline.

The Random Spacing (7-24 minutes)
The system adds random delays between 7-24 minutes to make emails look more human and less automated. This is intentional - it prevents Gmail from flagging you as a spammer.

Why "1 Mill" Goes First
The system pre-schedules ALL future emails for each person when you add them. So when you added "1 Mill" first:

Email 1: Nov 17 (today + 0 days + random minutes)
Email 2: Nov 21 (Nov 17 + 3 days + random minutes)
Email 3: Dec 1 (Nov 21 + 8 days + random minutes)
Email 4: Dec 11 (Dec 1 + 10 days + random minutes)
Then you added the other 49 stores. They ALL get their Email 1 scheduled for roughly the same time (Dec 11), spaced out by 7-24 minutes each to avoid rate limits.

The Problem You're Identifying
You expected FIFO across ALL emails (Email 1 to person A, Email 1 to person B, Email 1 to person C, then Email 2 to person A, etc.)

But the system does FIFO per step (All Email 1's first, then all Email 2's, etc.) within each recipient's personal timeline.

Is This a Defect?
Partially yes. The queue is showing you individual scheduled sends, but it's confusing because:

"1 Mill" appears 4 times because they have 4 steps pre-scheduled
