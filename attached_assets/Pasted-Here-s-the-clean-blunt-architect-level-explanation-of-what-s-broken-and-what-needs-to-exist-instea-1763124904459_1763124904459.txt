WHAT YOU HAVE NOW (BROKEN BY DESIGN)

	•	Your negotiation
	•	Your refinements
	•	Your rephrasings
	•	Your contradicting instructions
	•	Your half-formed drafts
	•	Your changes of mind

…all get reloaded into the system prompt every time the AI has to write a single outgoing email.

This is insane from an architectural standpoint because:
	•	It creates giant prompts
	•	It increases cost
	•	It confuses the AI
	•	It dilutes clarity
	•	It slows everything down
	•	It makes outputs inconsistent

Worse: you and the assistant are essentially building the AI’s prompt inside the prompt.
You are making a meta-prompt that keeps growing.

This is exactly the kind of recursive prompt-injection situation that breaks reliability.

HOW IT CURRENTLY WORKS (technical clarity)

When generating an email, the code literally does:

CAMPAIGN STRATEGY CONTEXT:
YOU: Mike says something
ASSISTANT: AI replies
YOU: Mike changes direction
ASSISTANT: AI updates
YOU: Mike corrects again
ASSISTANT: AI restates
...etc

This whole transcript is stapled to the system prompt on every generation.

So if you did:
	•	6 messages → ~1,000 extra tokens per email
	•	20 messages → ~3,500 extra tokens per email
	•	50 messages → ~8,000 tokens per email

Multiply that by:
	•	Sending 100 emails per day → 800,000 wasted tokens
	•	Sending 500 emails per day → 4 million wasted
	•	Sending 50,000 emails in a month → literal bankruptcy-level token burn

And worse:

The AI is forced to read your whole negotiation every time and guess which version is the “truth.”

WHY YOU’RE FEELING CONFUSED

Because what you are doing feels right (iterating strategy),
but what the system is doing with your iterations is wrong.

“Here’s 50 drafts from last week — read them all every time before you write the next email.”

THE FIX YOU NEED (and what Replit is already hinting at)

The correct architecture is:

1. You talk to the AI as much as you want → 5 iterations, 50 iterations, whatever.

2. When you’re happy, you click:

“Finalize Strategy”

3. The system summarizes the ENTIRE conversation into a single, crisp brief:

Something like:

CAMPAIGN STRATEGY:
- Target audience: US dispensaries in legal states
- Tone: founder-to-founder, craft, sustainable, veteran-owned energy
- Instruction: Each subject must be unique, each body must be unique, follow-up threading ok
- Constraint: Avoid spammy patterns, avoid hype, be human
- Value: White-label hemp wick, sustainability angle, handmade Poland production
- Etc.

4. This summary becomes the FINAL strategy prompt

stored as:

sequences.finalized_prompt

5. The old transcript is archived but NEVER injected again.

6. Every outgoing email uses:

SYSTEM PROMPT:
- base system instructions
- your finalized strategy brief (200–300 words)
- keyword bin
- product context
- recipient-specific personalization

That’s it.

	•	Stable email generation even at 1000+/day
	•	Predictable behavior across recipients

This is exactly how Apollo, Instantly, Outreach.io, Salesloft, etc. work behind the scenes.
