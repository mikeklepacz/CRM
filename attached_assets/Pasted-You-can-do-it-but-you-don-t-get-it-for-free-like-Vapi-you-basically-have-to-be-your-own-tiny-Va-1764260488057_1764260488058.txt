You can do it, but you don’t get it “for free” like Vapi – you basically have to be your own tiny Vapi and insert a mixing layer between Twilio and ElevenLabs.

Let me strip it down.

How Vapi is (almost certainly) doing it

They’re not asking Twilio to play background noise separately.

They’re doing this:
	•	User audio → Twilio → their server
	•	Their server forwards user audio to the LLM/TTS (ElevenLabs-type thing)
	•	LLM/TTS sends back clean agent audio
	•	Their server adds background noise to that audio (software mix)
	•	Mixed agent audio → back to Twilio → to the callee

So the noise is baked into the agent’s voice stream, not a third “noise call” in a Twilio conference.

You can copy that pattern.

Your stack version (Replit + Twilio + ElevenLabs)

Right now you probably have something like:

Twilio ↔ ElevenLabs (via Twilio <Stream> → ElevenLabs WebSocket)

To inject noise, you change it to:

Twilio ↔ Your Node server on Replit ↔ ElevenLabs

Your Node server becomes the “man in the middle”:
	1.	From Twilio:
	•	Receives the live audio from the customer (via Twilio Media Streams or <Stream>).
	•	Pipes that audio on to ElevenLabs as usual.
	2.	From ElevenLabs:
	•	Receives streaming TTS audio chunks for the agent.
	•	Before sending them back to Twilio, mixes in your office-noise loop at a low volume.
	•	Sends the mixed audio back to Twilio.

Result: the callee hears “agent + office hum” as one audio stream. ElevenLabs still hears the user clearly (you don’t add noise on the input side, only on the output back to Twilio).

No funky Twilio conferences, no third fake participant, no fighting with TwiML verbs.

Technically, that means:
	•	A small Node service that:
	•	Opens a WebSocket to Twilio (media stream)
	•	Opens a WebSocket/HTTP stream to ElevenLabs
	•	Keeps a buffer of your noise WAV in memory (or streams from disk)
	•	On each outgoing TTS chunk, does something like:
mixedSample = agentSample + (noiseSample * noiseGain)
with clipping protection
	•	Then wraps the mixed PCM/µ-law back into the format Twilio expects and sends it.

That’s basically what Vapi is doing for you under the hood.
