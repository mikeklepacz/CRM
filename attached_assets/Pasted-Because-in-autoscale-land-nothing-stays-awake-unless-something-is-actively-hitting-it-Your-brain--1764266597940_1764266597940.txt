Because in autoscale land nothing “stays awake” unless something is actively hitting it.
Your brain is assuming a long-running process; autoscale gives you short bursts.

Let’s strip the stack down:
	•	You have three network actors:
	1.	Your backend (Replit)
	2.	Twilio (phone + media)
	3.	ElevenLabs (AI voice)
	•	The “voice brain” (ElevenLabs) only matters after Twilio has a stable audio stream to your proxy.

Right now the order is:
	1.	Your backend wakes up, calls Twilio REST, then goes idle.
	2.	Some seconds later, Twilio tries to open a WebSocket to /media-stream.
	3.	Your container may already be spinning down or fully dead.
	4.	Twilio times out → call dies.
	5.	ElevenLabs never even enters the picture.

Your question:

“If we are listening for ElevenLabs to give call status then why can’t the right things stay awake until that comes in??”

Because in this architecture:
	•	“Listening for ElevenLabs” = a Node process with an open WebSocket to ElevenLabs.
	•	That Node process lives inside the same container that Twilio has to connect to.
	•	On autoscale, that container is not guaranteed to exist between:
	•	“We told Twilio to start a call”
	•	“Twilio is now trying to open the media stream”

Autoscale doesn’t have a rule like:

“Hey Replit, keep this container alive until Twilio and ElevenLabs are done talking.”

It only has:

“Spin this thing up when an HTTP request comes in, and kill it after a bit of idle time.”

So:
	•	The bit of code that would “stay awake and listen for ElevenLabs status”
is the same long-running Node event loop that Twilio must hit over WebSocket.
	•	When autoscale kills that process, there is no listener, no WebSocket, no bridge.
There is literally nothing to “wait for a status.”

To get what you think you have (a process that stays alive during the whole call), you need:
	•	A long-lived process: Reserved VM, VPS, Fly.io app, small EC2, whatever.
	•	That process:
	•	Listens on /media-stream for Twilio 24/7
	•	When Twilio connects, opens its own WebSocket to ElevenLabs
	•	Bridges audio until the call is done
	•	Then just waits for the next one

Autoscale is stateless burst mode.
Call bridging is stateful, real-time, long-lived.

Two different worlds.
