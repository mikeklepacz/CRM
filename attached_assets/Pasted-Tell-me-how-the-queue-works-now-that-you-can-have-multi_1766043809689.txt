The E-Hub queue system has evolved into a slot-first architecture that elegantly handles multiple sequences sending emails concurrently. Here's how it works:

Core Architecture
1. Slot Generation (Matrix2/slotGenerator.ts)

Pre-generates daily email "time slots" based on your settings (daily limit, sending hours, jitter)
Creates slots for 3 days ahead to ensure there's always availability
Each slot is a specific UTC timestamp when an email can be sent
Slots are sequence-agnostic - they don't care which sequence fills them
2. Priority-Based Assignment (Matrix2/slotAssigner.ts)

Runs every 60 seconds to assign eligible recipients to empty slots
Uses a 3-tier priority system:
Tier 1 (Highest): Manual Follow-Ups at step 1+ (human handoffs getting AI follow-ups)
Tier 2 (Medium): Active follow-ups at step 2+ (nurturing existing sequences)
Tier 3 (Lowest): Cold outreach at step 0 (first contact)
Recipients from ALL sequences compete for slots based on priority
Checks eligibility (timezone, business hours, step delays) before assignment
3. Queue Processing (emailQueue.ts)

Runs every 60 seconds to send emails whose slot time has arrived
Sends up to 10 emails per cycle
After sending, automatically assigns the recipient to their next step's slot (multi-step progression)
Includes Reply Guard - checks for replies before sending to prevent interrupting conversations
Multi-Sequence Coordination
The beauty is that sequences don't interfere with each other:

Key Features
Fairness: All sequences share the same slot pool, so no single sequence can monopolize sending capacity.

Rate Limiting: The daily email limit (e.g., 200/day) is enforced globally across all sequences automatically.

Intelligent Spacing: Jitter (min/max delay) creates human-like randomness between sends, regardless of which sequence they're from.

Multi-Step Progression: After a recipient receives an email, they're automatically assigned to a new slot for their next step (e.g., 3 days later), competing again in the priority queue.

Reply Protection: The Reply Guard checks Gmail threads before sending, preventing follow-ups when a conversation has already started.

Example Flow
Day 1, 9:00 AM:
- Slot opens
- 5 recipients eligible (2 from Seq A step 0, 2 from Seq B step 2, 1 from Seq C step 1)
- Priority: Seq C step 1 wins (Manual Follow-Up tier)
- Email sent to Seq C recipient
- Recipient auto-assigned to next slot (Day 4, considering step delay)
Day 1, 9:05 AM:
- Next slot opens
- 4 recipients eligible
- Priority: Seq B step 2 wins (Active Follow-Up tier)
- Email sent to Seq B recipient
The system ensures fairness, rate limiting, and priority-based delivery all work together seamlessly across unlimited sequences.
