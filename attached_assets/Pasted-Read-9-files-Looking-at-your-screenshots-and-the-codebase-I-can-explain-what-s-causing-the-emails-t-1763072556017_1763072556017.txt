The Issue
The problem is in smartTiming.ts's computeNextSendSlot function. When a recipient has business hours like "Daily 2am–9pm", the system should be scheduling emails starting at 3am (2am opening + 1 hour offset), but instead it's defaulting to noon.

Here's what's happening:

Your Company Time Range is 6am-11pm (6-23)
Your Client Time Range offset is 1 hour after opening, cutoff at 11pm (23)
The recipient's business hours parse as "Daily 2am-9pm" which should give a window of 3am-9pm in their timezone
However, when the business hours are "24/7" or poorly formatted, the code falls back to using noon as a safe default
Looking at the business hours parsing in timezoneHours.ts, the string "Daily 2am–9pm" should parse correctly, but the issue might be that it's being treated as a 24/7 business or the parsing is failing.

The Root Cause
In smartTiming.ts at the getRecipientWindow function:
